Analyzing the Polarization State of Light through the Fourier Series

(Experiment Code: 3.4)

Description

One can completely determine the polarization of light simply by using a polarizer and a quarter wave plate (QWP). In this experiment we developed a method to generate as well as analyze different polarization states of light.

Experimental setup

A HeNe laser beam is incident on a polarizer. The polarized light then passes through two QWPs, a neutral density filter and an analyzer. Finally it is received by a photodiode. By orienting the polarizer such that it produces a horizontal polarization. All angles can be measured with respect to this polarizer. The schematic diagram of this apparatus is shown in Figure 1.

Unit Intensity Plot

Now we generated polarization by rotating the first QWP to some angle α between 0◦ to 45◦ and then changing the angle β of the second QWP from 0◦ to 360◦ in some steps and recording the photodiode output for each step will give us unit intensity plot.
